///////// 改后
Voronoi diagram is one of the effective methods to generate areal data from point data. However, the patches of Voronoi diagram generated from large-scale geographical point data will appear severe spatial conflicts and visual overlaps, which reduces the visual communication of spatial attribute distribution, causing the further exploration to be hard. In this paper, we propose a Voronoi diagram generation method for large-scale geospatial point data combining spatial clustering and adaptive blue noise sampling, so that the final Voronoi results can maintain the spatial association characteristics and spatial distribution of attributes. Firstly, a clustering method (based on minimum spanning tree) is performed to divide the original data into different clusters, which represent the distribution in both spatial and attribute dimensions; Then, driven by the clustering results, a adaptive blue noise sampling method is used to simplify the original large-scale geographical point data set. Taking the sample as seed points, we generate the Voronoi diagram. In addition, we provide an open source visual analytic system for performing our method, displaying the diagrams and evaluating the results. A set of interaction design is integrated, facilitating users to more deeply explore and analyze the spatial characteristics of data. A series of case study, quantitative comparison and user study using real-world datasets further verify the effectiveness and practicability of our system tools.
